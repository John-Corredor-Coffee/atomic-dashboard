4
Cold Brew & Shipping Rules
Critical for ship accounts — prevents the #1 cold brew shipping ticket
Ship
Quick note on ordering Cold Brew + saving on shipping Snippet

One thing worth flagging before your first order: if your total is over $300, coffee beans, tea, and filters ship free via UPS ground — but the Cold Brew Concentrate is excluded from free shipping since it ships packed with insulation and cold packs.

The best practice is to order Cold Brew separately from everything else. That way you only pay the cold-pack shipping on the cold brew itself and keep the rest of your order free.

Everything is on the portal at serveatomiccoffee.com — you can place two separate carts to keep them split.

Don't skip this module for ship accounts. Cold brew shipping confusion is one of the most common new-account tickets — this single paragraph deflects most of them.
When to send Include in Module 1B for any ship account that carries cold brew. Can also be a standalone reply when they first ask about shipping charges.
5
Equipment & Espresso Quality
For espresso-based accounts — dial-in help + equipment support contact
Espresso
Troubleshooting snippet

A few things worth going through if you're dialing in:

1. Grind size — too fine = over-extracted (bitter). Too coarse = under-extracted (sour, weak). Adjust one click at a time.

2. Distribution — a quick Stockfleth's move before tamping helps prevent channeling.

3. Tamp consistency — aim for level and firm (~30 lbs) every time. An angled tamp is one of the most common culprits.

If you're having recurring consistency issues, the BOSeTamper ($325) is available on the portal — it self-levels automatically and is a solid alternative to a Puq Press (~$900+).
